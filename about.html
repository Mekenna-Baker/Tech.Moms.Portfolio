Intro

Born in Auburn, Washington, I was the youngest of four. However, that status was short-lived. After my parents' divorce, two cross-country moves, and settling down in Utah, I was bumped to middle child status. I grew up in a suburb of Salt Lake City, Utah, known as Sandy. I lived with my father, step-mother, older sister, and younger sister. My eldest 2 sisters resided in Indiana with my mother. Growing up we developed annual family trips. We would visit places like Moab, Gooseberry, Duck Creek, and California. These were trips that we looked forward to all year.

From the outside looking in, my upbringing probably seemed pretty normal. However, on the inside, it was full of physical, and emotional abuse, as well as neglect. When I graduated high school at Jordan High in 2015, my parents surprised me by selling our house and moving without me. I had assumed I was going along with them, but suddenly found myself staying with my grandma, who thankfully welcomed me with open arms. I soon got a part time job, and enrolled full time at Salt Lake Community college. In high school I had taken concurrent enrollement classes for criminal justice, and I had also been apart of Sandy City Polices' Explorer Program. This background combined with my upbringing motivated me to study social work, hoping to eventually become a social worker, and help prevent what happend to me, from happening to others. Unfortunately, my own health issues threw a wrench in my plans. I had to take breaks from school several times, and eventually, it became too much to handle financially, trying to cover both tuition and medical bills.

I met my husband, Kaden, in my first semester of college, alongside some of our other very close friends. Our friend group was always hanging out and doing something, and Kaden didn't show up very often. This led my best friend and I to try to include him more, and eventually we were hanging out all the time. We started dating at the end of 2016, and got married in 2019, just before we moved to Indiana later that year. We moved to Indiana so I could try to reconnect with my eldest sisters, and some of my other family from my mother's side. While we were out there, the pandemic hit, and the area slowly started to become more dangerous to be in. We moved back to Utah, and got back to mostly normal life. Within the next year or so, we found out we were expecting, and at the beginning of 2022, we welcomed our sweet son, Silas, to the world.
